Good Evening Team,


I'm excited to announce that the Trinity College Rocketry Club will be resuming this week. An email will be sent out in the next few days which will contain information about our first session and planned agenda.


Initially, we will keep the club's membership quite small temporally until planning has been completed, and parts arrive for the construction of our initial projects. This will ensure that we have activities, roles and documentation ready for new students.  


I have been working on the design for our first rocket (Name TBC at first meeting), launchpad, and briefcase launch controller system.

 

These projects have been scaled-down, when compared to our original plans, but will hopefully allow us to hone our skills, develop resources and attract funding for some of the more ambitious projects which will take place next year, like Active TVC.

 

The rocket will be constructed out of Quantum Tubing (120cm) and will include an electronics bay running an Ardunio/Teensy built inside the nose cone (This will allow us to record altitude, apogee, photos/video etc.).

 

This will make things easier by allowing us to use the ejection charge which comes preinstalled inside the motor, instead of having to develop a second-stage pyro ignited system as originally planned. Other ideas which we can explore include developing a deployable landing leg system using nichrome wire. 

 

 

The launchpad will feature a linear launch-lug system, with retractable arms powered by servo motors (see gif for inspiration). There is also the possibility of adding servo-powered clamps to the pad, allowing us to conduct static fire tests. 

 

The briefcase control system (see image below for inspiration) will allow us to have control over the entire launch process, including the launchpad via a transmitter (LoRa), and will ensure that we are compliant with all government and NAR safety codes. I will cover the plan for this during our first session. 

 

On another note, I hope everyone was able to tune into SpaceX's DM-2 Crewed Mission to the ISS. We are on the brink of a new era where becoming a multi-planetary species will soon become a reality. 

 

This term we will also spend some time looking at university degrees and career pathways into the space industry for anyone who is interested. 

 

I am looking forward to seeing everyone soon.
